## Why the hooks are shaped this way

Each one stacks **contrast + specificity**, the two levers that survive contact with a feed:

- **"Same bottle. Two states. ₹400 difference."** — the A (a bottle has a price) vs B (it has 36
  prices). Concrete number, no throat-clearing, topic clear in three words.
- **"Your 'one peg' is actually two drinks."** — `your` framing around a pain the viewer already
  has. They can't opt out; they've said "just one peg" out loud.
- **"Old Monk never advertised. Not once."** — implied contrast. Only B is stated; the A (brands
  must advertise) is supplied by the reader. Savvier, and it lands harder.

**What I deliberately avoided:** "Let's talk about India's alcohol taxes" (delay), "The shift
happening in India's alcobev industry" (fog), "Did you know alcohol isn't under GST?" (no stakes).
All are the reflex opener, and all die in the feed.

## The rules that hold this together

1. **One page per post. Always.** A carousel with no destination is a vanity post. Link in bio
   changes daily to match.
2. **Slide 8 is the source slide, every time.** "Source: Maharashtra Excise Policy 2025-26." It
   is the entire brand — see [01_BRAND.md](01_BRAND.md). It's also why people trust us enough
   to click through to a website.
3. **Repeat pages deliberately.** Days 8, 16, 28 revisit earlier pages from new angles. A page
   climbs Google because it keeps earning attention — one post never does it.
4. **Never invent a number.** If R1/R2 hasn't landed, use the ranges we can defend and say
   they're estimates. One fabricated price destroys the only thing we're selling.
5. **Reply to every comment in the first hour.** Cheapest reach multiplier that exists.
