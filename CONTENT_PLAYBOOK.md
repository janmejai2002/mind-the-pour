# Mind the Pour — Content Playbook

> Portable methodology so ANY agent (Antigravity/Gemini, etc.) can write on-brand, high-performing content without the Claude Code skills that produced the originals. Distilled principles, applied to this brand. Use this whenever writing hooks, captions, scripts, carousels, or explainers.

## Voice (non-negotiable)
Smart, dry-witted, data-driven, plain-English. Short sentences. One idea per line. We're fascinated by the *business*, never selling a buzz. 21+, responsible, never preachy. Every claim carries a number or a source (and if unverified, mark it — don't fake it).

---

## 1. Hooks — the first 1–2 seconds decide everything
A hook has one job: make the right person keep watching. It must do TWO things at once:
- **Topic clarity** — in ~2 seconds they know exactly what this is about.
- **On-target curiosity** — they feel it's for *them*, and an open question pulls them forward.

**The 4 ways hooks fail (diagnose + fix):**
1. **Delay** — topic arrives too late. Fix: delete all throat-clearing ("let me explain," "story time"). First words = the subject.
2. **Confusion** — needs a second read. Fix: fewer, simpler words; active voice; one idea per sentence.
3. **Irrelevance** — clear but not "for me." Fix: swap *I/my → you/your*; frame around a pain the viewer already feels.
4. **Disinterest** — clear + relevant but no open question. Fix: **contrast** — state the common belief (A) then your claim (B). The gap is the curiosity.

**Build a hook by stacking levers:** a pattern + an angle + a *specificity multiplier* (concrete/odd numbers, named things). Example stack (contrast + specificity): "Same bottle. ₹800 in Goa, ₹2,400 in Karnataka."

**Banned openers:** "let me explain," "let's talk about," "story time" (with no stake), "nobody talks about this," "wait till you see this," "[brand] just launched…," or a CTA as the opener.

**Always write 3 variants**, each a *different lever stack*, not synonyms. Read them aloud — spoken rhythm beats grammar.

## 2. Anti-AI writing — don't sound like a bot
Kill the tells that make text read as AI:
- No "In today's world…", "Let's dive in", "It's important to note", "game-changer", "unlock", "elevate", "in conclusion."
- No over-balanced "not only… but also," no triads-for-the-sake-of-it, no hedging everything.
- Vary sentence length hard — a 3-word line next to a longer one. Use fragments.
- Concrete > abstract. Name the thing. Prefer a real number or example over a general claim.
- Cut adjectives that don't carry information. If a sentence survives deletion, delete it.
- Read it aloud: if you wouldn't say it to a smart friend at a bar, rewrite it.

## 3. Storytelling — hold the watch after the hook (for talking-head / narrative pieces)
- Open a loop early ("here's the part nobody expects…") and close it at the end.
- Structure: hook → stakes (why it matters to *you*) → turn (the surprising bit) → payoff → button (one-line landing + CTA).
- Use a single concrete example over a general explanation. Specificity is credibility.
- Tension = a gap the viewer wants closed. Keep one open at all times.

## 4. Dumbify — make economics effortless to follow (for explainers/teaching)
- Assume zero background. Define the one term that matters, skip the rest.
- One concept per post. If there are two, make two posts.
- Replace jargon with the plain word ("excise duty" → "the state's tax on alcohol").
- Use a concrete rupee example instead of a formula.
- Analogy > definition. ("Pour cost is the bar's version of 'cost of goods'.")

## 5. Caption formula (every post)
1. Restate the hook in one line. 2. One surprising fact. 3. One "why it matters." 4. CTA (save/share/comment — pick ONE). 5. 3–5 tight, human hashtags (no hashtag soup).

## 6. Platform notes
- **Reel/TikTok**: hook = first 1–2s spoken + visual. Lead with the striking visual. Text-on-screen always (most watch muted). 20–35s.
- **Carousel**: slide 1 = hook in ≤9 words + a reason to swipe. Last slide = CTA.
- **Newsletter/YouTube**: subject line/title earns the open/click (curiosity + clarity); first line earns the read.

## 7. Pre-publish checklist
- [ ] Hook passes all 4 killers (delay/confusion/irrelevance/disinterest)?
- [ ] Reads like a person, not AI (Section 2)?
- [ ] One idea, effortless to follow (Section 4)?
- [ ] Every number verified or clearly marked unverified?
- [ ] Responsible framing, 21+, no glamorising?
- [ ] Brand voice: short lines, dry wit, receipts on the table?
